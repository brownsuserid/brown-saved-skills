# Brain Bridge Email Approach Frameworks

Five frameworks for structuring sales outreach based on deal context, buyer readiness, and relationship state. Select the approach before drafting.

---

## Approach A: The Insight Lead

Open with a specific industry insight or trend, connect to a pain point, offer a concrete resource, soft CTA.

**Best for**: Prospects in active industries with known pain points. Early-stage deals (Contacted, Responding).

**Structure**:
1. Specific industry insight or trend observation
2. Connect insight to a pain point the prospect likely faces
3. Offer a concrete resource (article, workshop invite, case study)
4. Soft CTA: conversation, not sale

---

## Approach B: The Peer Story

Reference an anonymized success story from their industry, highlight a specific outcome, bridge to their situation.

**Best for**: Prospects in industries where we have case studies. Mid-stage deals (Soft-Qualifying, Hard-Qualifying).

**Structure**:
1. Reference an anonymized case study from their industry
2. Highlight the specific, measurable outcome
3. Bridge: "Your situation with [X] reminded me of this"
4. CTA: explore relevance

**Requires**: A matching case study (see [bb-case-studies.md](bb-case-studies.md), match 2 of 3: industry, role, pain point)

---

## Approach C: The Direct Value

State the problem directly, quantify cost of the problem, share how we've solved it, offer to show how.

**Best for**: Prospects who have expressed friction or pain. Deals with identified pain points (Soft-Qualifying+).

**Structure**:
1. State their problem directly (from deal context/pain points)
2. Quantify the cost: what it's costing them in time, money, opportunity
3. Share how we've solved it (proof points or case study)
4. CTA: offer to show how

---

## Approach D: The Solo Pitch

Acknowledge pain of solo sales, share specific metrics, reference Week One Experience guarantee.

**Best for**: Solopreneurs, founders, small sales teams. Brain Bridge Solo prospects.

**Structure**:
1. Acknowledge the pain: "Sales by yourself truly sucks"
2. Share specific Solo metrics: cost per lead ($0.18-$0.34), week-one results, 5-minute campaign creation
3. Reference Week One Experience: leads targeted/researched/contacted, forgotten follow-ups happen, missed replies flagged
4. CTA: 30-min demo

**Key Solo details**:
- Pricing: $1,500 signing + $1,500 delivery + $492/year = $3,492 first year
- Operating costs: Most clients under $150/month
- Delivery: 45 days from deposit

---

## Approach E: The Free Offer (AI for Leaders)

Lead with free value, zero commitment. Lowest-friction CTA in the arsenal.

**Best for**: Cold outreach, stalled deals, new verticals, prospects not ready to buy. Any deal that has gone quiet or where direct sales pressure would backfire.

**Structure**:
1. Lead with free value: "Join our free weekly AI coaching"
2. Describe format: hands-on learning with Josh Brown, no pitch, no commitment
3. Position as ongoing resource, not a one-time event
4. CTA: join next session

**Three free offerings to reference**:
1. FREE AI COACHING (weekly sessions): Hands-on AI experience, group or 1-on-1
2. FREE AI EXPERT OFFICE HOURS: Direct access to AI specialists, practical answers
3. FREE AI REALITY CHECKS: Strategy sessions to pressure-test AI ideas

## Selection Guide

| Deal context | Recommended approach |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Cold outreach, no prior relationship | A (Insight Lead) or E (Free Offer) |
| Stalled deal, gone quiet 14+ days | E (Free Offer) or B (Peer Story) |
| Active deal with identified pain points | C (Direct Value) |
| Industry where we have a strong case study | B (Peer Story) |
| Solopreneur / small team prospect | D (Solo Pitch) |
| Re-engagement after long silence | E (Free Offer) |
| Post-meeting follow-up | C (Direct Value) or A (Insight Lead) |
| Warm intro from mutual connection | A (Insight Lead) |

When uncertain, default to **E (Free Offer)** for cold/stalled deals and **A (Insight Lead)** for warm/active deals. Never use D (Solo Pitch) for enterprise prospects.

## Objection Handling Reference

If the last interaction involved pushback, address it before re-engaging:

| Objection | Response angle |
|-----------|---------------|
| "We've already tried AI tools" | Methodology vs. technology. We build AI Teammates embedded in workflows, not standalone tools. |
| "How is this different from a consultant?" | We deploy working systems and stay to optimize. Not a report, a running AI system. |
| "What if AI replaces employees?" | Human-centered approach. AI handles routine work so people focus on judgment and relationships. |
| "We're not a tech company" | Best results are with non-tech: construction, staffing, education, manufacturing. |
| "What does this cost?" | Offerings at every level. AI for Leaders is free. Workshops from $3K. Solo $3,492/yr. Full builds ~$25K. |
| "How long before results?" | Workshops: day one. Solo: week one. AiOS: 30-60 days. |
| "Not ready to commit" | AI for Leaders, free weekly coaching, no pitch, no commitment. |
