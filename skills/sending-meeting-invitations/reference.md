# Sending Meeting Invitations

Write meeting invitations that get responses and set clear expectations.

---

## Required Elements

### 1. Subject Line (under 60 chars)

Include purpose and timeframe. No clickbait.

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "Meeting request" | "Intro call re: AI implementation - week of Feb 24" |
| "Quick chat" | "Follow-up: onboarding timeline discussion" |
| "Catching up" | "Q1 planning sync - 30 min" |

### 2. Purpose (one sentence)

Why this meeting matters to *them*, not you. Lead with their benefit.

- Bad: "I'd like to discuss our services"
- Good: "Want to share three approaches other companies your size have used to cut AI implementation time in half"

### 3. Agenda (3-5 bullets max)

Each item with time estimate. Total should equal meeting duration.

```
Agenda (30 min):
- Introductions (5 min)
- Your current workflow challenges (10 min)
- Potential approaches (10 min)
- Next steps (5 min)
```

### 4. Logistics

- **Duration:** State explicitly (15 min, 30 min, 45 min)
- **Scheduling:** Include Calendly link
  - Intro: `calendly.com/aaroneden/20min`
  - General: `calendly.com/aaroneden/1-on-1`
  - Sales: `calendly.com/aaroneden/1-on-1-call-45m-pri`

### 5. Sign-off

Name, title, and contact info. Keep it clean.

## Templates

### Intro Meeting (Cold/Warm Outreach)

```
Subject: AI implementation intro - [Company Name]

Hi [Name],

[One sentence on why you're reaching out, referencing something specific to them.]

I'd love to share how companies like [similar company] have [specific outcome].

Agenda (20 min):
- Quick intros
- Your current challenges with [topic]
- 2-3 approaches that might help
- Decide if there's a fit for a deeper conversation

Book a time here: [Calendly link]

Best,
Aaron Eden
Brain Bridge | AI Consulting
```

### Follow-up Meeting (Existing Relationship)

```
Subject: Follow-up: [topic from last meeting] - [timeframe]

Hi [Name],

Following up on our conversation about [topic]. Ready to dig into [next step].

Agenda (30 min):
- Recap decisions from last time (5 min)
- [Main topic] (15 min)
- Action items and owners (10 min)

Pick a time: [Calendly link]

Aaron
```

### Internal Sync (Team/Collaborator)

```
Subject: [Project] sync - [date or timeframe]

Hey [Name],

Time to align on [project/topic].

Agenda (30 min):
- Status updates (10 min)
- Blockers and decisions needed (15 min)
- Next steps (5 min)

Grab a slot: [Calendly link]

Aaron
```

---

## Anti-patterns

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Vague subjects ("Quick chat", "Touching base") | Gets ignored or deprioritized |
| No agenda | Signals poor planning, makes them do the work |
| "Let me know what works" without Calendly | Creates unnecessary back-and-forth |
| Wall of text | Respect their time, get to the point |
| All about you | They care about their problems, not your pitch |
| Multiple meeting options in body | Confusing, use Calendly instead |

## Guardrails

- **Draft first, send never** unless explicitly told to send
- Personalize the opening line. Generic = ignored.
- Always include Calendly. Never play email tennis with availability.
